Moiré lattices in twisted multilayer graphene

The ability to systematically assemble two-dimensional materials opens opportunities for designing highly tunable quantum states and electronic devices.

My research has focused on multilayer graphene, often incorporating rotational misalignment between the crystal lattice of neighboring atomic layers. This introduces a new length scale in the system - that of the moiré pattern. The family of such materials is known as twisted N-layer + M-layer graphene.

Superconductivity in twisted double bilayer graphene on WSe2

In a superconductor, electrons collaborate to form a macroscopic quantum ground state, allowing charge/current to flow without dissipation.

In this work, we discovered that stacking twisted double bilayer graphene (t2+2) on few-layer WSe2 stabilizes superconductivity in this material. Our findings underscore the remarkable influence of proximal substrate interfaces and the moiré potential in creating new ground states within van der Waals heterostructures. These results contribute to the growing body of evidence in graphitic systems, suggesting a correlation—though not an overlap—between electronic states with a tendency toward isospin polarization and those conducive to superconductivity.

Nature Materials 22, 1332-1337 (2023)

Recyling robot

This is an robot I designed for a competition to retrieve and deposit the greatest number of scattered soda cans into a recyling bin within 1 minute. Switching to online classes during this time meant the robot had to be constructed/soldered by hand.

I put together an IR detection circuit for line following, H-bridge circuit for motor control, and programmed a microcontroller in C++ to use a sonar module for soda can detection. This is a video of a prototype in action!

Machined phone holder

I machined this foldable phone holder from brass and aluminum scraps. Unlike typical plastic holders, it's big enough to hold a tablet, built to last, and heavy.

This was a project to become a qualified user of UBC's student machine shop. It involved using the mill, lathe, drill press, water jet, as well as hand tools such as the tap and die.
